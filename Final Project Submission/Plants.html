Plants that are Great to grow in Apartments

These are a few plants that are great for apartments that need low to medium light and also low to medium maintenance.

ZZ Plant ZZ Plant (Zamioculcas zamiifolia) is one of the toughest indoor houseplants around. The grow in low light. They don't need a lot of water, so do not overwater them.
Dracena There are so many variations of this plant. They prefer bright (indirect) sunlight and are a natural air purifier. But be careful, they are toxic to pets. Water less often in fall and winter than during the growing seasons of spring and summer. Fertilize monthly during the spring and summer with standard liquid plant food.
Monstera Deliciosa Known as the "Swiss Cheese Plant". These plants are beginner-friendly. Monsteras will thrive where they can get a good amount of indirect bright light. Only water your monstera when its soil is dry, this should be once per week. Make sure to keep your monstera under warm temperatures.
Snake Plant(Sanseveria) Its known as the “Mother-in-law’s Tongue". They have many variations of this plant. This plant is a very popular and tough indoor plant. Its adaptability makes it great for rooms with low, medium or high light levels. They don't need a lot of water, so do not overwater them.
Lucky Bamboo Lucky bamboo resembles bamboo, but it is actually a member of the dracaena family (dracaena sanderiana). Bamboo can be planted in water or soil. Bamboo are sensitive to the chemicals present in tap water, so it’s best to water your lucky bamboo plant with distilled water, bottled water, or with tap water that has sat out for 24 hours. Change water weekly. After every four weeks clean the water and container. If plant bamboo in potting soil, keep the soil moist, not wet for good drainage. Mist your bamboo a few times a week.
Pothos They are known as Epipremnum aureum family. They have many variations of this plant. Its a very ivy like plant they can grow very long. Pothos plants don’t like to be in too wet of soil for too long so you’re better off under-watering them than overwatering them. Its adaptability makes it great for rooms with low, medium or high light levels.

Vegetables/Fruits that grows well on Apartment Balcony/Patio

These are a few vegetable and fruit plants that have been great to grow in containers on apartment balcony/patio.

Lettuce Lettuce is a very hardy crop and one of the easiest vegetables to grow. They have many variations of this plant. It can be grown in a variety of climates and has the ability to withstand minor frost. Lettuce roots do not require deep soil and can be easily grown in shallow containers. They can grow in the sping and summer but they grow better in the fall/winter.
Collard Greens They also grow well in containers. They have many variations of this plant. They can grow in partial or full sun. They grow well in fall and winter also.
Tomatoes Tomatoes grow well in containers. They have many variations of this plant. Tomatoes can be grown in any sized containers. Make sure the container is large enough to handle the plant and you can begin with seeds or starter plants. Add a cage to the outside of the container for extra support as the plant gets taller. They grow well in the spring/summer time.
Beans They have many variations of this plant. Beans does very well in containers but the container needs to at least 12 inches deep for the best results. A pole is needed because the growth will travel up once it begins growing well. Determines which variety they can grow well from spring to fall.
Herbs Herbs like Oregano, Sage, Parsley and other herbs are great to grow indoors or outside on patio or balcony. They are low maintenance. Most of the herbs can grow in the same pot. The only herb that might grow well with other herbs are mint. Mint can grow wildly. They grow well in the spring/summer time but all year round indoors.
Strawberries Strawberries can actually grow very well in containers. When growing them you need to make sure its located in a very sunny location. You do need to choose a container large enough to handle them. They grow well in the spring/summer time.
